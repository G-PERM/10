Data Handling

See The Chapter PDF

Short Answer Questions(SAQs)

What is a double bar graph?

A double bar graph is used to display two sets of data on the same graph.

What is a scaling factor?

A scale factor is a number which multiplies (“scales”) a quantity.

What is the meaning of ‘Favourable outcome’?

A favourable outcome is an outcome that denotes how many times we expect the thing to happen.

MCQs

1. What is the range of the data: 10, 15, 20, 25, 30?

(a) 20

(b) 30

(c) 10

(d) 15

► (a) 20

2. Which graph is best to compare two sets of data?

(a) Line graph

(b) Pie chart

(c) Double bar graph

(d) Pictograph

► (c) Double bar graph

3. What is the median of the data: 2, 4, 6, 8, 10?

(a) 6

(b) 5

(c) 8

(d) 4

► (a) 6

4. If a coin is tossed once, what is the probability of getting heads?

(a) 1

(b) 12

(c) 14

(d) 0

► (b) 12

5. Which of the following shows data using pictures or symbols?

(a) Bar graph

(b) Line graph

(c) Pictograph

(d) Double bar graph

► (c) Pictograph

6. What is the mode of the data: 2, 3, 4, 3, 5, 3?

(a) 2

(b) 3

(c) 4

(d) 5

► (b) 3

7. Which of the following cannot be the probability of an event?

(a) 0

(b) 1

(c) 35

(d) 1.5

► (d) 1.5

8. In a bar graph, the width of bars must be:

(a) Unequal

(b) Equal

(c) Alternating

(d) Any size

► (b) Equal

9. What is the sum of probabilities of all possible outcomes of an event?

(a) 0

(b) 2

(c) 1

(d) 12

► (c) 1

10. Which central tendency is affected by extreme values?

(a) Mean

(b) Median

(c) Mode

(d) Range

► (a) Mean

11. What type of graph is used to show continuous data?

(a) Line graph

(b) Bar graph

(c) Pie chart

(d) Histogram

► (a) Line graph

12. How many faces does a standard die have?

(a) 4

(b) 6

(c) 8

(d) 10

► (b) 6

13. What is the range of 5, 10, 15, 25?

(a) 20

(b) 25

(c) 15

(d) 10

► (a) 20

14. A coin is tossed. What is the total number of outcomes?

(a) 3

(b) 4

(c) 2

(d) 1

► (c) 2

15. In a pictograph, one symbol represents:

(a) Always 1

(b) Always 10

(c) Varies as per scale

(d) Always 5

► (c) Varies as per scale

16. Mean of 5, 10, 15 is:

(a) 10

(b) 15

(c) 12.5

(d) 5

► (a) 10

17. Which is a possible probability value?

(a) −0.5

(b) 1.2

(c) 45

(d) 2

► (c) 45

18. If 2 dice are thrown, what is the maximum possible sum?

(a) 6

(b) 10

(c) 12

(d) 11

► (c) 12
