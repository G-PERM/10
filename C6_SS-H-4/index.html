What Books and Burials Tell Us

See The Chapter PDF

One of the Oldest Books in the World

→ The oldest book known worldwide is the Vedas.

→ They were composed around 3,000 years ago and are the earliest known literary source.

The Vedas

  • The word Veda means Knowledge.
  • There are four Vedas: Rigveda, Samaveda, Yajurveda, and Atharvaveda.
  • The Rigveda is the oldest, composed about 3,500 years ago.
  • The hymns were composed by sages and passed down orally for generations before being written.
  • The hymns praise gods such as Indra (god of warriors), Agni (god of fire), and Varuna (god of sky).

How Do Historians Study the Rigveda?

  • Historians study written sources to understand the past.
  • They analyze the Rigveda, which contains many hymns in dialogue form.

→ These dialogues help historians draw conclusions about ancient times.

Cattle, Horses and Chariots

  • Many prayers in the Rigveda are for cattle, children, and horses.
  • Horses were yoked to chariots used in battles.
  • Battles were fought for cattle, land, pastures, water, and people.
  • Part of the wealth was used in Yajnas (sacrifices) offered into fire for gods, including ghee, grains, and special animals.

Words to Describe People

Political Life

  • The Vedas give insight into political life of that age.
  • The head of the tribe was called Raja.
  • The Raja had no capital, no palaces, no army, and no authority to collect taxes.

Occupations

  • Main occupations included agriculture, cattle-rearing, chariot-making, pottery, jewellery-making, tanning, and metalwork.

Dasas or Dasyus

  • Alongside the Aryans who composed the Vedas, there existed another group known as Dasas or Dasyus.
  • They were considered slaves or opponents of the Vedic people.

Silent Sentinels — The Story of the Megaliths

  • Archaeologists believe that objects buried with skeletons belonged to the deceased.
  • In Brahmagiri, a skeleton was buried with 33 gold beads, 2 stone beads, and 1 conch shell, while another had only a pot.

→ This suggests social differences—some people were rich, others poor.

  • Sometimes megaliths contain more than one skeleton.

→ This indicates that members of the same family were buried in the same spot at different times.

  • Special burials took place at Inamgaon.
  • Animals were likely used as food during burial ceremonies.
  • Skeletal studies help identify dead bodies and understand burial practices.
